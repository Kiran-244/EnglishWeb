Literature and Language

Words are not just symbols; they are doors to emotions we cannot name…

My thoughts on literature and language are simple yet profound. Upon reading this, you may find one part of your mind echoing, “Was it not what I thought about this concept?” At certain moments, however, we just feel short of words to express what we truly feel.

When I was in school, I never dreamed of being an engineer or a doctor — although these are noble pursuits. Instead, I was naturally drawn toward literature and books. I would find my home in novels, exploring worlds far beyond my own, and sometimes dive into literary texts that challenged me to think differently.

Ostensibly, literature may appear intimidating, but as one goes deeper into this nectar of wisdom, one realizes how unaware we were for so long. Literature is not just about reading; it is about experiencing life through words, feeling the heartbeat of every character, and seeing reflections of ourselves in their struggles and triumphs.

For me, language is not merely a medium to communicate ideas; it is a bridge to the heart. While the heart cannot always speak directly, we can make it speak through carefully chosen words. This is why I chose English as my medium — though, of course, the language itself is only a tool. What matters is the authentic expression of thought and feeling.

Life is complex. Some people walk paths unaware of their destination, while others, like me, turn to books to gain insight. Many focus on memorizing facts or gaining intellect, while some attempt to navigate life by imitating others. Yet, no one can truly decode the essence of life. What I know is simple: we are human, and we must try to understand every side of the spectrum of existence.

Ultimately, literature and language teach us empathy, curiosity, and introspection. Through reading, we learn to listen, to imagine, and to grow. Every story, poem, or essay is a mirror and a window — reflecting our own experiences while opening us to new perspectives. In the end, the beauty of literature lies in its ability to connect hearts and minds across time, space, and culture.
