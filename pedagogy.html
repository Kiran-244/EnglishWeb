Pedagogical Curiosity

Teaching isn't just about teaching but also about learning...

Teaching, which in itself I believe is a humble yet eloquent profession, has always fascinated me. But sadly, today we come across many teachers who entered this field by chance rather than choice. For me, becoming a teacher was a conscious decision, driven by curiosity and passion.

One might think of a teacher as someone who simply walks into a classroom with a stack of papers or a textbook, asks questions, conducts tests, gives assignments, and repeats the same routine endlessly. I firmly believe that teaching cannot be that boring. Although I might appear routine at times, earnestly, I strive to explore new ways to help students learn more effectively and seamlessly.

Teaching is always a two-way process. When you stand at the lecture stand in front of students from diverse backgrounds, religions, and ways of thinking, it gives you a perspective that is truly enriching. Initially, one might feel butterflies in the stomach, but gradually, speaking to an audience of learners becomes natural and rewarding.

My journey as an Assistant Professor has immersed me in an environment that provides a wider perspective — educational, social, and emotional. Teaching is not just about sharing knowledge; it is also about learning. Every interaction, every question, every discussion is an opportunity to grow.

My curiosity as a teacher drives me to constantly explore new methods and approaches to connect with students, to inspire them, and to create a learning experience that is meaningful and memorable.
